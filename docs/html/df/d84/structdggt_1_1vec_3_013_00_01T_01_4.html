template<typename T >
vec< 3, T > dggt::vec< 3, T >::cross ( const vec< 3, T > &  rhs) const

Cross product.

The cross product of two vectors is a vector perpendicular to both vectors in the direction determined by the right-hand rule. (ie. Hold your hand so your fingers can curl in the direction from the left hand side to the right hand side. Your thumb is pointing in the direction of the cross product.). The length is determined by the product of the length's of each vector multiplied by the sine of the angle between them.

Parameters
rhsThe right hand side of the cross product.
Returns
The cross product of the two vectors.

The hadamard product.

The hadamard product of two vectors is another vector with elements that are simply the product of the two vector's corresponding elements. (eg. given v0=[x,y] and v1=[s,t] then v0.hadamard(v1)=[x*s,y*t].). These are often used for color blending.

Linearly interpolates two vectors weighted by t.

A linear interpolation is a kind of blending between two values assuming one transforms continuously and linearly into the other as a parameter, t, varies. When t=0 the return value is this vector and when t=1 the return value is b and everything in between.

Parameters
