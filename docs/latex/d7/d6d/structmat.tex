Matrix multiplication. 

Multiplies two matrices, lhs$<$\+C,\+R,\+T$>$ and rhs$<$\+R,\+M,\+T$>$, to produce a product, S$<$\+C,\+M,\+T$>$ where S=lhs$\ast$rhs. Matrix multiplication is defined such that the i\textquotesingle{}th row and the j\textquotesingle{}th element of the product is equal to the dot product of the left hand side\textquotesingle{}s row vectors with the right hand side\textquotesingle{}s column vectors. (ie. S\mbox{[}j,i\mbox{]} (columns written first) is equal to lhs\+\_\+row\mbox{[}i\mbox{]}.dot(rhs\+\_\+col\mbox{[}j\mbox{]}) where lhs\+\_\+row and rhs\+\_\+col are the i\textquotesingle{}th row of the left hand matrix and the j\textquotesingle{}th column of the right hand matrix, respectively.). \begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
In general A$\ast$\+B!=B$\ast$A for matrices A and B. 
\end{DoxyNote}
